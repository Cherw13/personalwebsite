Redesign of NARY's website & email newletters

As a digital designer, I work to redesign NARY's new website interfaces concentrating on UI design. I also worked on graphic design of their weekly emails

Duration: 2 months (solo)

Strategies: UI design, Graphic Design, Responsive Design

Link: Naryresort.com

See Final Prototype

Context

NARY is an ethical and sustainable resort wear brand based in Phnom Penh, Cambodia and NYC. Their mission is to seek to end the cycle of human exploitation in Cambodia by transforming the lives of survivors with safe long-term employment and compassionate support. Additionally, their commitment extends to revolutionizing the fashion industry by producing high-quality garments that address both social and environmental issues while putting an end to labor exploitation

I was assigned the responsibility of redesigning their website, with a primary focus on enhancing the user experience for customers, particularly on the homepage and clothing purchase page. The goal was to create a seamless and welcoming shopping experience, allowing NARY customers to truly embody the NARY brand.

Project Details

Design Goal

To improve the overall user experience, inspiring customers with the confidence to wear NARY clothing; and secondly, to refine the design of the clothing purchasing page, ensuring a seamless and effortless shopping process and overall enhance branding impact.

Target Users

  • - 25 to 80 years old
  • - women who you see lives in tribeca or upper brooklyn
  • - pregnant people


UX Redesign



The orginal NARY website has really good visual design but it has some user experiences problems and new features to add to display the clothings items in more inviting friendly way

  • - menu items was not immediately visible when the page loads. Users cannot see clothing categories or options right away. Requiring them the effort to locate and access them.

  • - Primary interactions are not prioritized in the purchasing page

  • - Clothing items are disorgranized and needed a new way to group them

  • - needed to simplify and condense some informations for easier and faster navgiation
